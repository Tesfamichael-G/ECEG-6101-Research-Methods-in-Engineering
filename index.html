Course Overview

This course provides graduate students with the fundamental skills necessary to conduct rigorous engineering research. It covers the entire research lifecycle: from identifying research problems and conducting comprehensive literature reviews to designing experiments/simulations, analyzing data, adhering to ethical standards, and communicating findings.

Key Competencies

  • Formulate clear, testable research problems in Communication Engineering.
  • Conduct systematic literature reviews using IEEE Xplore, Scopus, etc.
  • Design robust simulation-based or experimental methodologies.
  • Apply statistical analysis to interpret engineering data.
  • Adhere to professional research ethics (Plagiarism, Data Integrity).
  • Develop a high-quality Thesis Proposal.

Assessment & Grading

40%

Research Proposal

A complete thesis proposal (10-15 pages) including problem statement, lit review, methodology, and work plan. Includes a 15-minute oral defense.

30%

Critical Article Review

A 2-3 page critical analysis of a recent (last 3 years) high-impact IEEE journal article. Focus on methodology critique, not just summary.

30%

Written Final Exam

Closed-book exam testing conceptual understanding of research design, ethics, statistics, and methodology.

Policy

  • Attendance: 80% mandatory. Missing >20% results in failure.
  • Late Submissions: 10% penalty per day.
  • Academic Integrity: Zero tolerance for plagiarism.

Ethics Case Study: "The Perfect Plot"

Scenario: Alex, an M.Sc. student, runs simulations for his RIS beamforming algorithm. His average result (17.9 bps/Hz) is slightly worse than the state-of-the-art (18.2 bps/Hz). However, one specific random seed gave him 19.1 bps/Hz. He considers reporting only that best seed to impress his supervisor.

Discussion Questions

  1. Is reporting the "best-case" seed without context ethical? Why or why not?
  2. What is the difference between Fabrication and Falsification?
  3. How should Alex present his results honestly if they don't beat the baseline?
