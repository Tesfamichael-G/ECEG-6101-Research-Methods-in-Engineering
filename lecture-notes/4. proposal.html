Research Proposal Structure & Guide

Proposal Paper Structure

  • Title Page
  • Introduction (The "Why" & "What")
  • Literature Review (The "Who")
  • Methodology (The "How")
  • Work Plan & Budget (The "When" & "Cost")
  • References

1 Title Page

Your title must be descriptive and concise. Avoid vague terms like "A Study of..."

Formula: [Method/Algorithm] for [Problem/Application] in [Context/System]

Example: "Deep Reinforcement Learning for Energy-Efficient Beamforming in RIS-Assisted 6G Networks"

2 Introduction

This section answers What?, Why?, and So What?. Use the Hook-Gap-Goal framework.

2.1 Background & Problem Statement
  • The Hook: Briefly introduce the broad field (e.g., "Massive MIMO is critical for 5G...").
  • The Gap: Identify the specific problem (e.g., "...but existing algorithms suffer from high latency.").
  • The Goal: State your proposed solution (e.g., "This research proposes a low-complexity algorithm...").
2.2 Research Questions & Objectives

Translate your goal into SMART objectives.

Criteria Example
Specific "Design a user-pairing algorithm for NOMA..."
Measurable "...to improve sum-rate by 15%."
Achievable "Using MATLAB simulations..."
Relevant "...to support massive IoT connectivity."
Time-bound "...within the 6-month thesis period."
2.3 Significance of the Study

Who benefits? Explain the Theoretical (new model/framework) and Practical (cost/energy reduction) importance.

3 Literature Review

This section answers Who has done similar work?. It shows you are not reinventing the wheel.

  • Cite Key Papers: Mention 5-10 seminal works in your field.
  • Identify Gaps: Categorize gaps as Evidence, Knowledge, or Methodological.
  • Define Terms: Clarify technical vocabulary (e.g., "RIS," "Beamforming," "NOMA").
💡 Tip: Don't just list papers. Synthesize them: "While Smith et al. focused on X, they did not address Y, which this study will tackle."

4 Methodology

This section answers How?. It must be detailed enough for another engineer to replicate your work.

4.1 Research Design
  • Approach: Simulation-based, Experimental, or Analytical?
  • Tools: MATLAB, NS-3, Python, HFSS, USRP?
  • Data: Synthetic data, Public datasets, or Lab measurements?
4.2 System Model (Engineering Specifics)
  • Topology: Single-cell? Multi-cell? User distribution?
  • Channel Model: Rayleigh, Rician, AWGN, 3GPP UMi?
  • Hardware: Antenna count (MIMO), RF chain constraints?
  • Signal Processing: Modulation, Coding, Detection algorithms?
4.3 Performance Metrics

Define how you will measure success: BER, Throughput, Latency, Energy Efficiency, Complexity (FLOPs).

5 Work Plan & Budget

This section answers When? and How Much?.

5.1 Timeline (Gantt Chart)
Phase Activity Duration Deliverable
1 Literature Review & Proposal Defense Month 1-2 Approved Proposal
2 System Modeling & Algorithm Design Month 3-4 Simulation Code v1.0
3 Data Collection & Simulation Runs Month 5-6 Raw Data Sets
4 Analysis & Interpretation Month 7 Results Chapter Draft
5 Thesis Writing & Final Defense Month 8-9 Final Thesis
5.2 Budget & Resources

List common cost items: Software Licenses (MATLAB/CST), Hardware (USRP/Antennas), Cloud Computing (AWS), Publication Fees (APC).

💡 Tip: If using university labs, state: "The research will utilize existing university facilities. Estimated incidental costs are $X for printing and conference registration."

6 References

List all cited works in IEEE Format.

Example:
[1] Q. Wu and R. Zhang, "Towards Smart and Reconfigurable Environment: Intelligent Reflecting Surface Aided Wireless Network," in IEEE Communications Magazine, vol. 58, no. 1, pp. 106-112, Jan. 2020.
