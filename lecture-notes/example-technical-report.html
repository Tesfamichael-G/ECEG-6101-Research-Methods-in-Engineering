TITLE & ABSTRACT

Deep Learning-Based Channel Estimation for Massive MIMO Systems in 6G Terahertz Bands

Alex Johnson, Student Member, IEEE, and Dr. Sarah Chen, Senior Member, IEEE

Abstract

💡 Structure: 1 sentence context + 1 sentence problem + 1 sentence method + 1 sentence result + 1 sentence implication

Context: Terahertz (THz) communication is a key enabler for 6G networks, promising Tbps data rates. Problem: However, accurate channel estimation in THz Massive MIMO systems is challenging due to severe hardware impairments and extreme channel sparsity. Method: This paper proposes a convolutional-transformer neural network (CT-Net) that jointly learns spatial and temporal correlations from pilot signals under realistic RF impairments including phase noise and IQ imbalance. Result: Simulation results demonstrate that CT-Net achieves 18.3% lower NMSE compared to traditional MMSE and 12.7% improvement over recent deep learning baselines at SNR = 15 dB, while maintaining inference latency below 1 ms. Implication: The proposed method enables robust beamforming for practical 6G THz deployments with non-ideal hardware.

Index Terms: 6G, Terahertz communications, Massive MIMO, Channel estimation, Deep learning, Hardware impairments

1. INTRODUCTION
💡 Funnel Approach: Broad context → Specific problem → Your solution → Contributions

1.1 Background

The exponential growth in mobile data traffic demands spectral efficiencies beyond the capabilities of sub-6 GHz bands. Terahertz (0.1–10 THz) spectrum offers vast bandwidth for 6G networks [1]. Massive MIMO with hundreds of antennas compensates for severe THz path loss through narrow beamforming [2].

1.2 Problem Statement

Accurate Channel State Information (CSI) is critical for beamforming. However, existing estimators (LS, MMSE, OMP) assume ideal RF chains [3]. In practice, THz transceivers suffer from phase noise, IQ imbalance, and power amplifier nonlinearities [4], causing significant NMSE degradation.

1.3 Contributions

💡 List 3-4 specific, measurable contributions
  1. We propose CT-Net, a hybrid CNN-Transformer architecture for THz channel estimation under hardware impairments.
  2. We develop a realistic impairment model combining phase noise, IQ imbalance, and quantization errors for THz Massive MIMO.
  3. We demonstrate 18.3% NMSE improvement over MMSE with inference latency < 1 ms, suitable for real-time beamforming.
2. SYSTEM MODEL

2.1 Network Topology

We consider a single-cell downlink Massive MIMO system with a base station (BS) equipped with Nt = 256 antennas serving K = 16 single-antenna users.

2.2 Channel Model

Received signal:
y = Hx + n

where:
H ∈ ℂK×Nt is the channel matrix
x is the transmitted pilot vector
n ~ CN(0, σ²I) is AWGN

The THz channel follows a clustered multipath model [5]:

H = √(Nt/L) Σl=1L αl ar(θl)atH(φl)

2.3 Hardware Impairment Model

We model phase noise as a Wiener process [6]:

θ[n] = θ[n-1] + Δθ, where Δθ ~ N(0, σ²θ)

5. DISCUSSION
💡 Answer: Why? Comparison? Implications? Limitations? Future work?

5.1 Interpretation

The superior performance of CT-Net stems from its ability to capture both spatial correlations (via CNN) and temporal dependencies (via Transformer) in the channel, which traditional methods ignore.

5.2 Comparison with Prior Work

Unlike DCE-CNN [7] which uses only convolutional layers, our hybrid architecture reduces overfitting with limited training data. The 12.7% improvement validates the importance of modeling temporal correlation.

5.3 Practical Implications

With inference latency < 1 ms, CT-Net is suitable for real-time beamforming in 6G systems with coherence time ~10 ms.

5.4 Limitations

💡 Be honest about limitations—this strengthens credibility
  • Training requires GPU acceleration (~6 hours on NVIDIA V100)
  • Performance degrades under extreme mobility (>120 km/h) due to rapid channel variation

5.5 Future Work

  • Extend to multi-cell scenarios with inter-cell interference
  • Investigate federated learning for privacy-preserving distributed training
6. CONCLUSION

This paper proposed CT-Net for THz Massive MIMO channel estimation under hardware impairments. Simulation results demonstrated 18.3% NMSE improvement over MMSE with real-time inference capability. The method enables robust beamforming for practical 6G deployments.
